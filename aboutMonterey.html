Dive into the stunning marine life of California’s coast at the iconic Monterey Bay Aquarium.

The Monterey Bay Aquarium is one of the most renowned aquariums in the world, located on the historic Cannery Row in Monterey, California. Opened in 1984, the aquarium sits right on the edge of the Pacific Ocean and is uniquely designed to draw water directly from Monterey Bay, giving many exhibits a living, natural ecosystem. It’s famous for showcasing the rich marine biodiversity of California’s coastal waters, from playful sea otters to mesmerizing jellyfish.

One of the highlights is the Open Sea exhibit — a massive tank that features tuna, sharks, sea turtles, and sardines swirling in enormous schools. Equally awe-inspiring is the kelp forest exhibit, one of the first of its kind in the world, replicating the dynamic underwater forests found off the California coast. The aquarium was also the first to successfully exhibit and release young great white sharks, contributing to global shark research.

More than just a public attraction, the Monterey Bay Aquarium is a global leader in ocean conservation and marine research. Through initiatives like Seafood Watch, they educate millions about sustainable seafood and advocate for healthier oceans. With interactive exhibits, hands-on tide pools, and breathtaking coastal views, the aquarium offers an inspiring and unforgettable marine adventure for all ages.
