Dive into Denmark’s largest aquarium, where architectural beauty meets the wonders of the aquatic world.

The Blue Planet Aquarium, known locally as Den Blå Planet, is Northern Europe's largest and most stunningly designed aquarium. Located near the Copenhagen Airport in Kastrup, it features a distinctive whirlpool-inspired architectural design that appears to ripple into the surrounding landscape. Since opening in 2013, it has captivated visitors with its cutting-edge exhibits and immersive marine experiences.

Inside, the aquarium houses thousands of animals across several themed zones, such as the Ocean Tank, Coral Reef, and Northern Lakes. One of the highlights is the Ocean Tank—a massive 4-million-liter display with hammerhead sharks, rays, and swirling schools of fish. Visitors can also walk through a glass tunnel and witness marine life swimming above and around them, offering an unforgettable underwater journey.

The Blue Planet isn’t just about entertainment; it also serves as a center for marine research and education. Its mission focuses on spreading awareness about aquatic biodiversity, environmental threats, and the importance of ocean conservation. Whether you're admiring vibrant reef fish or learning about Arctic species, The Blue Planet offers a breathtaking gateway into the mysteries of the underwater world.
