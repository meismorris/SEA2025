Experience the wonders of marine life in Boston's historic and innovative New England Aquarium.

The New England Aquarium is a world-class marine science center and popular attraction located along Boston’s scenic waterfront. Since opening its doors in 1969, the aquarium has become a hub for marine education, ocean conservation, and family-friendly experiences. It’s especially well known for its Giant Ocean Tank, a massive Caribbean coral reef habitat that rises four stories and hosts hundreds of marine animals, including sea turtles and tropical fish.

Beyond its exhibits, the aquarium is deeply involved in marine research and rescue. It plays a critical role in protecting sea turtles and marine mammals through its Animal Care Center and rescue programs. Visitors can also engage with hands-on exhibits, from tide pools where they can touch sea stars, to engaging IMAX films that explore the depths of the ocean.

The aquarium is not just a place to visit — it’s an experience that educates, inspires, and connects guests to the beauty and complexity of the ocean world. Whether you're watching playful sea lions, walking through penguin exhibits, or boarding a whale-watching cruise, the New England Aquarium offers an unforgettable journey into marine life.
