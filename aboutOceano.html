Europe's largest aquarium, Oceanogràfic blends organic forms with futuristic design to create an immersive underwater experience.

The Oceanogràfic Aquarium, located in the City of Arts and Sciences complex in Valencia, Spain, is the largest aquarium in Europe. Designed by renowned architect Félix Candela and completed in 2003, it spans over 110,000 square meters and holds more than 42 million liters of water. The facility is dedicated to showcasing the world’s main marine ecosystems, making it both an educational hub and a popular destination for marine life enthusiasts.

The aquarium is divided into different thematic zones that represent distinct aquatic environments, such as the Mediterranean, the Arctic, the Antarctic, tropical seas, wetlands, and the Red Sea. One of its most iconic features is the 70-meter-long underwater tunnel that allows visitors to walk beneath swimming sharks and rays, offering a 360-degree view of life under the sea. The architecture itself reflects the fluidity of water, with curved white structures that give the impression of waves or shells.

Oceanogràfic is home to more than 500 species, including beluga whales, dolphins, sea lions, jellyfish, and numerous types of fish and birds. It also operates as a center for marine research and conservation, participating in breeding programs and promoting environmental awareness. Educational programs and interactive experiences are offered to school groups and the general public, helping visitors understand the importance of protecting our oceans.
