Explore global aquatic habitats, from rainforest rivers to deep sea wonders, at the National Aquarium.

TThe National Aquarium, located in Baltimore’s Inner Harbor, is one of the United States’ most celebrated aquariums. Since opening in 1981, it has become a centerpiece for marine education, conservation, and immersive experiences. With more than 20,000 animals representing over 750 species, it offers a deep dive into ecosystems from around the globe—ranging from tropical rainforests to arctic waters.

A standout feature is the Atlantic Coral Reef exhibit, home to vibrant marine life like angelfish, moray eels, and sea turtles. Visitors can also walk through a living rainforest habitat suspended above the main floor, where birds, monkeys, and reptiles coexist in a lush canopy. Another popular attraction is the Blacktip Reef, a massive floor-to-ceiling tank filled with sharks, rays, and a replica of a Pacific reef.

Beyond the exhibits, the National Aquarium is deeply committed to ocean conservation, animal rescue, and environmental stewardship. It runs numerous programs aimed at preserving aquatic ecosystems and inspiring the next generation of marine scientists and advocates. Through education and awe-inspiring design, the aquarium encourages all visitors to take part in protecting our planet's waters.
