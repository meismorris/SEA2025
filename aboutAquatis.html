Explore Europe’s largest freshwater aquarium, where ecosystems, science, and design meet beneath one roof.

Aquatis Aquarium-Vivarium is Europe’s largest freshwater aquarium and a leading destination for exploring aquatic biodiversity. Located in Lausanne, Switzerland, this visually striking and eco-conscious facility opened its doors in 2017 and quickly became a hub for environmental education and scientific curiosity. Its sleek, futuristic architecture reflects the innovation inside—where technology meets natural wonder.

The aquarium features immersive exhibits that showcase freshwater habitats from across five continents, including the Rhône, the Amazon, and the Congo River. Visitors can journey through thematic zones that highlight the evolution of life in rivers and lakes, with over 10,000 fish, reptiles, amphibians, and plants. Interactive screens and dynamic lighting make the experience engaging for all ages, bridging education and entertainment seamlessly.

Beyond its exhibitions, Aquatis emphasizes conservation and sustainability. It participates in breeding programs for endangered species and collaborates with global researchers on aquatic ecosystems. More than just an aquarium, Aquatis serves as a living museum of freshwater life, offering a thoughtful and awe-inspiring look at the world’s vital—but often overlooked—waterways.
