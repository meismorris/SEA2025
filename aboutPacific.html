Explore the Vast Marine Life and Ecosystems of the Pacific Ocean in an Interactive Learning Experience.

The Aquarium of the Pacific, located in Long Beach, California, is one of the largest aquariums in the United States. Opened in 1998, it spans approximately 20,000 square meters and is designed to represent the diverse marine life of the Pacific Ocean. The facility is home to more than 12,000 animals across 500 species, organized into exhibits that reflect different habitats including Southern California, Baja, the Tropical Pacific, and the Northern Pacific regions.

Visitors can explore a variety of interactive and immersive exhibits, including the Shark Lagoon, Lorikeet Forest, and the June Keyes Penguin Habitat. The aquarium emphasizes hands-on learning, allowing guests to touch marine animals like stingrays and sea cucumbers in designated touch pools. In addition to animal exhibits, the aquarium features multimedia displays and educational programs to raise awareness about marine conservation and the environmental challenges facing our oceans.

The Aquarium of the Pacific is not only a destination for family outings and school field trips, but also a hub for ocean advocacy, sustainability research, and community engagement. It regularly hosts forums, lectures, and cultural festivals that connect science with the public. With its scenic location by the Long Beach waterfront and its dedication to both entertainment and education, the aquarium continues to inspire curiosity and respect for the natural world.
